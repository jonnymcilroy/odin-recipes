Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its creamy, rich sauce made from eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper. Traditionally prepared with spaghetti, the hot pasta is tossed with the raw egg mixture, which creates a velvety sauce without the need for cream. The pancetta adds a savory, smoky flavor that perfectly complements the creamy texture, while freshly ground black pepper adds a subtle kick. Carbonara is loved for its simple ingredients and bold, comforting flavors, making it a popular and satisfying choice for pasta lovers.

Ingredients

  • Oil
  • Guanciale
  • Spaghetti
  • Eggs
  • Cheese
  • Seasonings

Steps

  1. Cook the pork in olive oil until browned and crispy, then drain on paper towels.
  2. Boil the spaghetti in salted water. Drain and return to the pot. Let cool.
  3. Whisk the eggs, 1/2 of the cheese, and some pepper in a bowl until smooth.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta, stirring quickly, until creamy.
  5. Stir in the pork, then top with the remaining cheese and more black pepper.
